Islamic Knowledge

Etiquettes of Dua

Begin with praising Allah and sending blessings upon the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). Face the Qibla, raise your hands, and ask with sincerity and certainty that Allah will respond.

Best Times for Dua

The last third of the night, between Adhan and Iqamah, during rainfall, while traveling, while prostrating in prayer, and on Friday after Asr until sunset.

Conditions for Acceptance

Dua is more likely to be accepted when accompanied by good deeds, halal sustenance, and a heart free from negligence. Avoid haste and have firm belief in Allah's response.

How to Make Dua

Speak with humility, lower your voice, use comprehensive phrases, and make dua for yourself before others. Repeat your request three times and end with praise of Allah.

What to Avoid in Dua

Avoid making dua for harm against others, for sin, or for severing family ties. Don't be impatient for a response, and never consider your dua too insignificant.

Dua in the Quran

Allah says: "Call upon Me; I will respond to you." (Quran 40:60). The Quran contains numerous examples of prophetic duas that we can incorporate in our own supplications.
